Let’s talk about the calendar …

Once you have logged on you should come the “Home” page for the maintenance area.  You will see a set of tabs across the top and some links on the home page.  I’ll talk about the tabs and the like in a later email, for right now, I want to direct your attention to the first grouping of links; specifically the “Calendar” link.  The calendar serves as a very important part of your website, and if you keep it up to date, I think that you find that it is one of the main reasons that YOUR chapter members start visiting your site frequently.  At first it will be like pulling teeth to get your members to go to the website, but all of a sudden you will find that they are using it more and more.  For Chapter-B, it was the calendar that started pulling people in.  We have even had people visit our chapter because of our website and our calendar!  Okay, enough justification; when you click on the Calendar link it will open a new browser window.

The Calendar Maintenance page lists all of the events for a specific section (or quite literally a file).  There is a drop-down list at the top of the page in the middle.  The first entry that you will see is “Chapter events”.  This section is for those events that you will enter as unique to a month.  These are non-repeating events.  They can actually be any kind of events that you want, but the way that I have structured things is that you have one section for events that you create and delete as each month rolls by.  Then there is another section titled “Chapter eventz2” (okay I was tired when I came up with the name), for those chapter events that happen monthly or annually.  Notice that I said “chapter events”, there is another section for district events.  By the way when you select a different entry in the drop-down list the page automatically reloads with the corresponding calendar entries.  Before I go into editing, I want to point out something that is pretty nice concerning the calendar.  There are a bunch of the sections that you don’t have to worry about maintaining at all.  They are automatically imported from the district calendar.  If you select the “District Events” section from the drop-down list you will notice that the listing is a little bit different.  First it doesn’t have any “edit” or “delete” links on the left-hand side and second it has a couple of extra lines of information above all of the calendar entries.  The first states “Entries Imported from external source”, the second identifies that it came from the district web server http://Alabama-gwrra.org/ and that it pulled from the calendar section “district_events”, then it goes on to tell you that your copy will be automatically refreshed every 14 days and on the transition of the month.  It is also worth mentioning that when you select the “Clear Cache” link on the maintenance home page that will also force the external calendar sections to be retrieved again.  It is worth noting that just because these items show up in your calendar does not mean that your chapter has elected to participate.  That is the reason that they are in a sort of gold/reference color.  If you are going to participate you should still add an event to the “Chapter Events” section giving details about how your chapter is participating.

Now let’s go back to the “Chapter Events” section and click on the “Add New Event” link in the upper-left corner.  I’m going to tell you about how “I” suggest using the calendar.  In some cases you can do what you like, but in other cases you MUST do what I am talking about (I will try to remember to tell you when I am making a suggesting versus a rule). 

SUGGESTION: Most of the time I just use the Subject and Body fields for typing information about an event.  I will usually do something like “Supper at Blah” or “Little River Canyon Ride” for the subject.  The subject generally is short and to the point.  The first thing that I usually put in the Body field is the Time that something will happen followed by a single dash and then the action that happens at that time.  Such as “8:00am – Depart from Moores Mill/Shell” or “6:00pm – 123 University Pky”.  By the way, I have found that if you identify times as “8:00am” instead of “8:00 a.m.” things work a whole lot better.  The reason is that you don’t have to worry about the “a.m.” or “p.m.” word-wrapping onto the next line without the time.  And as long as you use a lowercase for your “am/pm” it still is very readable.

Just to let you know the difference between the different fields and what happens regarding the formatting:

Subject will be output using the style defined by the event.  Body will follow the subject separated by a double dash (“ -- “) using the event style plus italic.  Location will follow the Subject and Body enclosed within parenthesis using the event style.  Comment will start a new line using the event style.

STRONG SUGGESTION: You really do need to specify a style for every event that you create.  I have setup a pretty good range of styles that are setup to flag members as to what the event is.

MUST DO: Categories are not required, but if you specify a category they should always be lowercase.  It also doesn’t make much sense to specify categories that you can’t search on … so the following are the categories that are used for the search tabs, the home page, or for the email notification.

SUGGESTION: I only specify a time in the time field for chapter events.  Today, the time field is only used for sorting events within a single day.  The way sorting works is that first any event that has a time specified is sorted (meaning all of those events will be listed first for that day), then you can look at your drop down list to get a feel for the order that things will get sorted by.

If you have a multi-day event, put the starting day as the event date, and then specify the duration.  The duration includes the starting day.  My suggestion on the "Expand Duration View" drop-down is to select "Month at a Glance" for any multi-day events.  If you want to see the difference, try creating a couple of events with different Duration Views.  Then look at them in the normal calendar tab (which is a List View) and the Month at a Glance to see the difference.

The last thing that I will say about the calendar for now is that when you change the calendar, you will frequently not see the changes on the homepage unless you remember to select "Clear Cache".
